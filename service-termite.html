How They Get In

Termites exploit every vulnerability.

Eastern subterranean termites travel underground and enter homes through the smallest gaps — often in places homeowners never think to check. We inspect every one of these points.

🏠

Foundation Cracks

Hairline cracks in concrete or block foundations are the most common entry point for subterranean termites.

🚪

Garage Doors

Wood door frames and the gap between concrete slab and siding are frequent termite access zones.

🔧

Plumbing Penetrations

Where pipes pass through foundation walls or slabs, gaps provide direct soil-to-wood paths.

❄️

A/C Lines & Conduit

HVAC conduits and electrical penetrations through exterior walls create hidden entry corridors.

🏗️

Soffit & Fascia

Damaged or aging soffit allows termites to access roof framing without touching the ground.

🌬️

Crawl Space Vents

Improperly screened vents and moisture in crawl spaces create conditions termites actively seek.

🌿

Landscaping Contact

Mulch, wood planters, or landscaping timbers in direct contact with the foundation are a welcome mat.

🪵

Attached Wood Structures

Wood decks, porches, or steps in contact with soil provide easy access to structural framing.

Our Process

Five steps from inspection to protection.

  • Step 1 — Full Property Inspection: We inspect all accessible wood, foundation walls, crawl spaces, and exterior perimeter for termite activity, mud tubes, and damage.
  • Step 2 — Assessment Report: Findings documented clearly — what we found, where, severity, and recommended treatment options. No pressure; just information.
  • Step 3 — Treatment Application: Liquid termiticide applied to create a continuous chemical barrier in the soil around the foundation — the colony contacts it traveling to and from the structure.
  • Step 4 — Monitoring: Bait stations or monitoring devices placed at key perimeter points to detect any renewed termite pressure.
  • Step 5 — Annual Re-Inspection: Annual inspection confirms the colony has not recovered and the treatment barrier remains effective.

Termite Service FAQ

Termite swarms in Nebraska typically occur March through May, when reproductive termites leave established colonies to start new ones. However, termites feed year-round — they simply slow down in winter. The spring swarm is your most visible warning sign, but interior colony activity never fully stops. Annual inspections ensure year-round awareness.
In nearly all cases, no. Standard homeowner's insurance policies explicitly exclude termite damage on the basis that it's a preventable pest problem — not a sudden, unexpected event. This makes professional inspections and treatment a critical investment. The cost of annual monitoring is a fraction of what structural repairs cost after termite damage is discovered.
Liquid termiticide treatments typically provide 5–10 years of soil protection under normal conditions. However, we recommend annual inspections regardless — soil disruption from landscaping, plumbing work, or foundation shifts can create gaps in the barrier. Our monitoring programs ensure continuous protection rather than relying solely on a single application's lifespan.
