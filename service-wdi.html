The Inspection

What we look for and how we look.

🏠

Exterior Assessment

Full perimeter walk of the property — foundation walls, crawl space vents, siding, window and door frames, wood-to-soil contact points, and attached structures (decks, porches, garages).

🔦

Interior Assessment

Basement and crawl space inspection using flashlight and probing tool — accessible floor joists, sill plates, posts, and all wood structural members within reach. We tap, probe, and look.

📝

Detailed Reporting

A standardized WDI report documenting every area inspected, findings for each wood destroying insect species, and clear notes on evidence of active or previous infestation — lender-ready format.

WDI Inspection FAQ

Most residential WDI inspections take 45–75 minutes depending on property size, access, and what's found. We work efficiently to keep your closing on track. Reports are typically delivered the same day or next business day. If you're on a tight closing timeline, tell us when you book — we'll prioritize accordingly.
The WDI report documents what was found — evidence of active infestation, previous damage, or conducive conditions — and we'll explain what it means in plain language. Lenders typically require treatment before closing if active infestation is found. As a full-service pest company, we can provide treatment and re-inspection to satisfy lender requirements without switching providers or losing time.
Either party can order the inspection, but it's most commonly ordered by the buyer or their lender as part of the due diligence process. Some sellers order a pre-listing inspection to identify and resolve issues before putting the home on the market — this can streamline negotiations and prevent last-minute delays. We work with buyers, sellers, real estate agents, and lenders directly.
