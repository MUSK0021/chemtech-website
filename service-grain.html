How It Works

The grain fumigation process.

1

Bin Assessment

We assess the bin's condition, seal integrity, and aeration system. We probe the grain for insect activity, temperature anomalies, and moisture levels to determine if fumigation is warranted and plan the treatment.

2

Sealing

The bin is sealed — roof vents, aeration openings, and access hatches are covered and secured to maintain lethal fumigant concentrations throughout the exposure period.

3

Fumigation

Aluminum phosphide tablets or pellets are introduced into the grain. As they react with moisture, they generate phosphine gas that penetrates the full grain mass over the exposure period (typically 3–7 days depending on temperature).

4

Aeration & Clearance

After the exposure period, the bin is aerated to purge residual gas below safe re-entry levels. We confirm clearance with gas detection equipment before releasing the bin for access. Complete documentation is provided.

Grain Fumigation FAQ

No. Phosphine fumigation is approved by the USDA and EPA for use on stored grain intended for human consumption and livestock feed. After proper aeration, no detectable residue remains. Grain treated with phosphine meets all U.S. elevator acceptance standards and export tolerances. We provide documentation confirming treatment dates and methods for your records.
Fumigation is most effective when grain temperatures are above 40°F — below this threshold, phosphine generation slows and exposure periods must be extended significantly. Fall fumigation (October–November) is common before grain cools for winter storage. We also fumigate in spring when temperatures warm and insects resume breeding. We'll assess your specific situation and recommend timing accordingly.
Signs of infestation include: visible insects in or on the grain, webbing or clumping at the grain surface (Indianmeal moth), hot spots detected during temperature monitoring, off odors, or grain that crumbles rather than feeling firm. If you're seeing moths near the bin at dusk, the infestation is already significant. When in doubt, call us — we'll probe the grain and give you a honest assessment.
