Why Heat Treatment Works

You have to kill every life stage.

Bed bug populations are dominated by hidden life stages — eggs and nymphs — that chemical sprays can't reliably reach. Heat penetrates everywhere and kills all of them.

Heat Treatment

Rooms are raised to 120–135°F — the lethal threshold for all bed bug life stages. Heat penetrates furniture, mattresses, walls, and baseboards. Our preferred method.

  • Kills eggs, nymphs, and adults
  • Often completed in a single treatment
  • No chemical residue
  • Can return home same day
🧪

Chemical Treatment

Residual insecticides applied to harborage areas — mattress seams, baseboards, furniture joints — combined with crack-and-crevice treatments and monitoring.

  • Lower upfront cost
  • Residual protection between visits
  • Requires 2–3 treatments
  • Works well for moderate infestations
Our Process

From inspection to clearance.

1

Inspection

We inspect every room, starting from the sleeping area and moving outward — mattress seams, box springs, furniture joints, baseboards, and wall outlets. We confirm the infestation and assess severity.

2

Prep Guidance

We provide a clear prep checklist so treatment is as effective as possible — reducing clutter, bagging electronics, washing linens. We walk you through every step before the appointment.

3

Treatment

Heat equipment is set up and the room temperature is raised to lethal levels and held there for the required duration. A chemical perimeter treatment is applied simultaneously for added protection.

4

Follow-Up

A follow-up inspection confirms complete elimination. For chemical treatments, we return for additional applications. We don't call the job done until you're bed-bug free.
