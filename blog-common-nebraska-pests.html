Nebraska's Top 5 Most Common Household Pests

Tips · By the Chem-Tech Team · Summer 2025 · 7 min read

Eastern Nebraska's climate — hot, humid summers, cold winters, and abundant agricultural land — creates conditions that support a wide variety of pest species. After 40+ years serving homes across 31 Nebraska counties, the Chem-Tech team has seen every infestation imaginable. Here are the five we encounter most often, ranked by the threat they pose to your family and property.

#1 — Termites: The Silent Destroyers

Threat level: Extreme — structural damage, no visible warning.

Eastern subterranean termites are Nebraska's single most destructive household pest. They live underground in colonies of tens of thousands and tunnel up through the soil into the wooden structure of your home — eating 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Because they work inside walls and beneath floors, significant damage can occur before any surface sign appears.

Nebraska homeowners lose thousands of dollars annually to termite damage that isn't covered by standard homeowner's insurance. An annual WDI inspection is the most important pest-related investment a Nebraska homeowner can make.

What to watch for: mud tubes on foundation walls, hollow-sounding wood, swarmer wings near windows in spring.

Chem-Tech Tip

Termite swarms in Nebraska typically occur March through May. If you see winged insects emerging from the soil or inside your home during this window, call immediately — you're looking at an active, established colony.

#2 — Rodents: Mice & Rats

Threat level: High — structural damage, disease, fire hazard.

House mice and Norway rats are year-round problems in Nebraska, but activity surges dramatically in fall as temperatures drop and rodents seek shelter indoors. A mouse can squeeze through a gap the size of a dime; rats need only a quarter-sized opening.

Rodents gnaw constantly to wear down their ever-growing teeth — including through electrical wiring, pipe insulation, and structural wood. Chewed wires are a leading cause of house fires attributed to "unknown origin." Beyond structural damage, mice and rats contaminate food and surfaces with urine and droppings, spreading diseases including Salmonella and Hantavirus.

What to watch for: droppings along walls and in cabinets, gnaw marks on baseboards or food packaging, nesting material (shredded paper, insulation) in quiet corners.

#3 — Cockroaches

Threat level: High — disease, allergens, rapid reproduction.

German cockroaches are the dominant indoor species in Nebraska and among the hardest pests to eliminate. They breed explosively — a single female and her offspring can produce thousands of roaches in just a few months. They spread 33 types of bacteria (including E. coli and Salmonella), 6 species of parasitic worms, and are a leading cause of childhood asthma attacks.

German roaches are almost exclusively indoor pests, travelling between apartments and homes through wall voids, plumbing, and shared spaces. American cockroaches favor basements and utility areas and are more likely to enter from outside.

What to watch for: roaches in kitchen or bathroom at night, musty odor, small dark droppings resembling coffee grounds, egg cases (oothecae) behind appliances.

#4 — Bed Bugs

Threat level: High — psychological distress, rapid spread, very difficult to eliminate without professional treatment.

Bed bugs have rebounded to levels not seen since the mid-20th century. They travel easily through used furniture, luggage, clothing, and even school backpacks — making any Nebraska home vulnerable. They're not a sign of uncleanliness; five-star hotels report infestations regularly.

While bed bugs don't transmit disease, their impact on quality of life is severe. The psychological distress of knowing something is feeding on you at night — combined with the difficulty and cost of elimination — makes early detection critical.

What to watch for: rust-colored stains on bedding, shed skins in mattress seams, small dark spots on walls and furniture, clusters of itchy red bites on exposed skin.

Act Fast

Bed bugs reproduce faster than most pest populations. A small infestation caught early can be eliminated in 1–2 treatments. Wait six months and you may be facing a whole-home treatment across multiple rooms.

#5 — Spiders

Threat level: Moderate — most are harmless, but two species in Nebraska require caution.

Nebraska is home to dozens of spider species. The vast majority — wolf spiders, cellar spiders, orb weavers — are harmless and actually beneficial, as they prey on other insects. However, two species found in Eastern Nebraska demand respect: the Brown Recluse and the Black Widow.

Brown recluses are found in dry, dark, undisturbed areas: basement storage, attic boxes, shoes left in closets. Their bite causes necrotic tissue damage and can require medical treatment. Black widows are found in garages, wood piles, and crawl spaces; their venom is neurotoxic and dangerous to children and the elderly.

High spider populations indoors also indicate a larger underlying pest problem — spiders follow their prey. A significant spider infestation suggests an abundance of insects they're feeding on.

What to watch for: webs in corners, undisturbed storage areas, and utility spaces; egg sacs (silk balls) in dark corners; red hourglass marking (Black Widow) or violin-shaped marking on back (Brown Recluse).
